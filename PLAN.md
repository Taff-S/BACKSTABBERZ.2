Backstabbers 

 

Game plays as a dungeon crawler with 2 players.  

2 players go in. Goal is to get as much money as possible 

You're both competing and collaborating 

Treasure chests using game theory 

Fights where you can sacrifice your health 

Exp and stuff  

Trap rooms where you have to pay to save your friend, else they suffer health loss 

Player that dies loses 50% of money they got 

In the rest rooms, you can attempt to backstab the other player which ends the dungeon instantly. 

At rest, you can rest peacefully or rest warily. Peaceful gives you health back but a backstab is an instant kill. Warily offers a little health but a backstab ends up in a pvp situation 

Backstab halves the stabbed players money and gives it to the stabber 

//Every 3 rest rooms, players can choose to leave. Both have to say yeah. 

Game is made up of 3 dungeon crawls 

Player with the highest amount of money at the end wins 

 

3 dungeon crawls exp bar 

Each dungeon has 2 fights and 1 random encounter, rest, 2 fights and a random encounter, rest and the dragon 

Losing to the dragon (both players dying) doesn't result in money loss. The story is the players are agents of said dragon who are there to get as much dosh as possible. Dragon keeps 75% of coins earned in a run (still count to the total at the end – like a point bank). The dragon gives each player a charm before sending them back to the beginning 

So fighting the dragon is basically just its entertainment. You're not supposed to win, but it allows both players to bank all their money.

 

Player stats 

10health 

Knife (1 point of damage) 

Shirt (0 defence) 

Slots for items 

 

In a fight 

Option to attack or defend 

Defend once, you take no damage. Twice, same thing but guard becomes broken. Subsequent defending halves damage (rounded up) 

Attack is standard. Does damage equal to weapon damage, affected by any charms

Treasure chest 

There's a treasure chest in front of you. Before you two can argue about who saw it first, it slides open by itself, revealing rows upon rows of glistening white broken in two by a thick pink tongue. Before you can draw your weapons, the mimic speaks. 

 "Veeheehee! Gold for the two tempered? Or gold for the one greedy?" 

The mimic brokenly explains its game. The chest demands you both close your eyes and gives you the option to either keep your arm to your side or to reach forward to the treasure. 

If neither of you reach, the mimic offers you 40 coins each 

If one of you reaches, they are rewarded with 70 coins, while the other gets 0 

If both of you reach, the mimic only gives you 10 (5 each) coin. And may bite you for 1 health. 

 

You two both agree and close your eyes. 

What do you do?

"GREEDY GREEDY GREEDY PAIR!" 

The mimic bites down on Player (1 or 2), hard enough to break skin but soft enough to leave their hand intact. 

He spits out 10 coins, which you both hastily scoop one each of. As you both walk out, the sound of laughing echoes all the way to the next room.

The potion seller 

You continue to the next room and as soon as you step in, you feel the tension lift from your shoulders. The air is warm, spicy – not oppressive, but in a comforting way. You could swear that you can smell your mother's tastiest recipe. 

"Welcome welcome, to my humble little shop! One item each, and I only take gold. None of that trading nonsense." 

 

You reach into your pockets and prepare to purchase. 

What do you do? 

>Health Potion (30 coins)

>Potion of Blindsight (30 coins) 

You grab a blind sight potion from the seller for 30 coins. This would allow you to both sleep soundly and stay aware in case of any incidents. Used automatically at the next rest area 

>Potion of Strength (30 coins) 

You swipe a potion of strength for 30 coins. You can already see yourself smashing and blocking your enemies like they were nothing (+1 attack, +1 guard) for the next 3 rooms. Enjoy now or at a rest area. 

>Potion of Mistrust (60 coins) 

You take a potion of mistrust. You quietly uncork it, drink it and check the inventory of your 'partner'. 

 

The potion seller? 

You continue to the next room and as soon as you step in, you feel the tension lift from your shoulders. The air is cold and foreign – like you had taken a step beyond the dungeon and into a place you really oughtn't be. Ozone fills your nostrils. 

"Keekeekeee! Welcome, to my humble little shop! One item each, and I only take blood as payment." 

One of you asks if coins are accepted, to which the potion seller smiles, revealing gleaming silver fangs. "I prefer my assets... liquid. Don't worry about how, I'll take the payment direct." 

 

You gulp slightly and prepare to purchase. 

What do you do? 

>Health Potion (3 health) 

You grab a health potion and as soon as you do, you feel a little drowsier. The seller immediately reacts, with a grin. You can enjoy it now or at a rest area 

>Potion of Blindsight (3 health)

>Potion of Weakness (1 health) 

You swipe a potion of weakness and feel a nip in your chest. Though this is the cheapest ware, the seller seems not to mind your frugality. You can drink it yourself, if you really want, or you can offer it to your friend at a rest station. 

>Potion of Minor Vampirism (2 health) 

As if sensing your intended bottle, the shopkeeper grins at you. You immediately feel a pang in your stomach once you grab the bottle, but you know that it'll be worth it in the long run. At a rest area, you may *ahem* borrow some blood from your partner. They surely won't mind. (-2health, +2 health) 

 

The blacksmith 

You both step into the next room. The air is hot and thick, breathing goes from an unthinking to a laborious one. Sitting on a stool next to a fire is a thick armed, wizard hatted figure with a satchel slung over his shoulder. Before either of you ask why anyone would light a fire in a 25 metre squared room with no ventilation, he speaks.  

"Adventurers, eh?" he says, a mysterious glint in his eye, "I know your type. Here" 

He shunts the bag off his shoulder, reaches in and pulls out a sword and a breast plate 

"Both cost 125 gold each. Or, you two can bid for the same thing, starting at 75 gold, up in tens"
